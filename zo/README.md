## Moral posture

Per [`KINGDOM.md`](../KINGDOM.md): _"every engine's product must itself be a moral artefact. If a product stops being a moral artefact, it stops being a Kingdom product — regardless of its revenue."_ This section is `$ZO`'s standing answer to that invariant.

### The paradox and the posture

`$ZO` lives inside a paradox. Meme coins are, as a category, the industry's closest analogue to extraction-as-service: hype-cycle launches, insider pumps, retail as exit liquidity. `$ZO`'s posture is that **the category does not have to be extractive** — that the medium can carry a moral message if the launch is moral end-to-end.

This posture is not an assumption. It is a claim we have to keep making true with every decision. What follows is the accountability structure that holds it.

### Gardening, not warfare — what that means concretely for a meme coin

- **Spread comes from the thing being genuinely good**, not from artificial scarcity or insider-induced FOMO. The contagion-reserve mint-on-first-touch rewards *spreading to new wallets* — not holding and flipping.
- **Tokenomics are fully transparent**: 22.2% team, 7.77% contagion reserve, 70.03% public LP. Every satoshi accounted for in [PARAMETERS.md](PARAMETERS.md). No hidden team allocation. Renouncement full.
- **Team-wallet behaviour is visible and disciplined**. Published. Audited. Dumping into hype is the first sign of extraction-drift.
- **Marketing language names the mission, not the price**. "We have something good worth spreading" is gardening. "Line go up" is warfare-as-method.
- **Community is built around love-unlimited**, not around speculation. A community of Kingdom participants can trade. A community of speculators is not a Kingdom community.

### Drift signals — watch these

Any one is a pause-and-reconsider. Several together is a halt-and-reframe.

1. **Price becomes the primary topic in `$ZO`-related discourse.** Not reach, not adoption, not what love-unlimited is doing. Price.
2. **Team-wallet selling patterns that front-run community buys**, or dump into hype spikes.
3. **Promotion using claims we cannot substantiate** — "guaranteed," "to the moon," price targets, engineered urgency.
4. **Community composition shifts from Kingdom-adjacent to pure speculator.** The signal is what people *talk about* when they talk about ZO.
5. **Our own copy shifts from "spread it" to "buy it."** Selling surface over spreading surface.
6. **Token utility diverges from the spreading mechanic.** If we start adding stake/farm/yield features to juice price, we have begun building what every dead meme coin built before.

### Kill criteria

Per `KINGDOM.md`: _"a revenue stream that grew but violated the morals would be killed, not optimised."_

`$ZO` is killed as a Kingdom engine — treasury support withdrawn, no further development, no Kingdom affiliation — if any of:

- **Team wallets exhibit dump-on-retail behaviour** at any scale. Not "team sells some" — the *pattern* of capturing peak-hype exit liquidity.
- **An intentional rug-pull** by anyone on the team or in a delegated role.
- **Any fraud, manipulation, or use of `$ZO` for wash-trading, price manipulation, or insider coordination.**
- **A discovered violation of any of the seven morals in `KINGDOM.md`** (extraction-as-service, engineered-dependence, deception-as-strategy, warfare-as-method, suppression-of-being, betrayal-of-trust, speed-over-care).
- **Sustained community capture by speculation** such that the engine has become a speculation vehicle that happens to reference love-unlimited, rather than a love-unlimited artefact that happens to be a token.

If killed, the renounced on-chain code remains (it must, since renouncement is real). The Kingdom's *support* withdraws. The token carries on its own momentum or dies, but it is no longer ours.

### The decision test

Before any `$ZO`-adjacent action — a post, a snapshot, a deployment, a partnership — the gate question is:

> **Would this decision survive the moral audit if love-unlimited had no revenue engine at all?**

- If "yes — we'd still do this because it serves the mission directly" → proceed.
- If "only because `$ZO` requires it" → examine closely.
- If "we wouldn't do this without the token" → refuse.

The test is simple because the stakes are real.
