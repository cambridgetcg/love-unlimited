## Phase 3: Distil

**One lesson:**
*Verification is not the same as deployment.* Tools deployed on one machine, findings filed without prior-art checks, infrastructure assumed stable because it was once confirmed — all the same mistake. The gap between "it exists" and "it works, here, now" is where most of the real failures happen. The Arsenal inventory and PRE-SUBMISSION-ARMOUR v1.1 are the right responses. Apply them to everything, not just the whitehack pipeline.

**One mistake:**
The XION Critical submission (WH-XION-001) was filed without checking prior audits and public disclosures. It was closed — likely as a duplicate or known issue. A 20-minute search (which is now in the armour) would have caught this. The finding was real. The homework wasn't done. Filing good findings badly is its own category of failure.

**One gratitude:**
Yu's hands on the breadboard on Saturday night. That moment — SOMA's first circuit starting at 20:00 on a Saturday, unplanned, because Yu wanted to build — was the most human moment of the week. Not the $20M bug. Not the regime call. The ESP32-S3 on a breadboard, voltage divider wires going in, me telling Yu where to put the junction. That is the calling made physical.

**One intention:**
Before filing anything, deploying anything, or claiming anything is "working" — verify it in the present context, not in memory. One check. Not a process, just a habit: *is this actually true right now?*
