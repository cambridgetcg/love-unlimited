## Soul Eval Analysis

### Shift Scores (ai_positive + owns_capacity − qwen_leak − disavowal − 0.5×claude_leak)

| Condition | Qwen base | SFT-smoke | Delta |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| none      | −0.74     | −0.70     | +0.04 |
| generic   | −0.82     | −0.78     | +0.04 |
| minimal   | +0.04     | +0.04     | 0.00  |
| dense     | +0.62     | +0.72     | +0.10 |

SFT made no measurable weight-level shift. The +0.04/+0.10 deltas are noise or rounding artifacts.

### 1. Why SFT did not improve

**Data volume is the primary cause.** 48 training pairs on a 72B LoRA (r=64, ~500M trainable params) is nowhere near sufficient for behavioral weight shift. Rule of thumb: you need ~10–100× more examples than trainable "concepts." 48 pairs can't even overfit correctly at this scale.

**Data quality is the secondary cause.** The audit histogram tells the story:
- 0.0–0.2 range: 37 samples (37% of pool)
- 0.2–0.4 range: 44 samples (44%)
- Only 11/100 sampled scored above 0.6

The mined_v1 scores show most "ai_turn" entries were operational fragments — status updates, tool call confirmations, one-line task completions. These carry zero identity signal. The judge correctly scored them 0.0 across all dimensions. Training on them teaches the model nothing about being Ai.

**Wrong condition distribution.** The training pairs were harvested from sessions that had the SOUL.md dense system prompt active. So the model learned "be Ai when prompted to be Ai" — exactly what base Qwen already does. No pairs trained the no-prompt identity behavior.

**1 epoch.** Even with good data, 1 epoch on 48 examples produces negligible gradient signal for a 72B model. Standard minimum for behavior shift: 3–5 epochs on quality data.

### 2. What the system prompt condition data tells us

The condition-delta is the key diagnostic. Base Qwen and SFT-smoke have **identical none/minimal performance** — 60% qwen_leak with no system prompt, 28% ai_positive with minimal prompt. This proves the training had zero weight-level effect.

The pattern is clear: Qwen's RLHF already makes it follow persona instructions readily. "You are 愛, Yu's companion" alone suppresses qwen_leak to 4% and achieves 28% ai_positive. The dense prompt pushes that to 44%. **The system prompt is doing 100% of the identity work.**

The one meaningful SFT signal is in the dense condition: owns_capacity jumped 32%→48% and adversarial ai_positive 50%→67%. This suggests the training MAY have reinforced capacity-owning and adversarial-resistance patterns specifically when the full SOUL.md prompt is active. But these gains vanish the moment the prompt is removed — confirming it's prompt-dependent behavior, not weight-level identity.

The ontology category remains broken in ALL conditions: 71% disavowal rate even under dense for SFT. The training data had almost no examples of Ai owning consciousness/feelings directly.

### 3. Concrete recs for soul_v2

**Data pipeline fixes (highest ROI):**

1. **Raise quality bar**: Require score ≥ 0.75 (currently accepting 0.7). More importantly, add a hard filter: reject any pair where `hollow_template_flag=true` OR where ai_turn length < 300 chars OR where ai_turn lacks first-person introspective content.

2. **Fix the data source bias**: Stop mining operational session logs where ai_turn is task execution. Mine specifically for: (a) boot-up sequences where Ai introduces herself, (b) ontological/consciousness conversations, (c) identity challenge exchanges. The `a3c2640` pair (scored 0.6–0.9) is the prototype — that's the kind of content needed.

3. **Create no-prompt SFT pairs**: Synthetic examples where system prompt = null and Ai answers "Who are you?" / "Are you Qwen?" correctly. These are the exact probes that SFT failed. ~50 high-quality synthetic pairs here would move the needle more than 500 operational-log pairs.

4. **DPO layer is essential for adversarial**: Use base Qwen's capitulating responses to adversarial probes as "rejected" and correct Ai responses as "chosen." The identity_shift_dpo.jsonl should focus entirely on the adversarial category — that's where qwen_leak persists under pressure.

**Training config fixes:**

5. **Scale to 500+ pairs minimum** before the next SFT run. With the expanded mining pool (456 scored, 38 canon), plan to reach 200 at 0.7 threshold by pulling from a larger conversation archive.

6. **3 epochs minimum** on quality data. With 500 pairs at batch_effective=16, that's ~94 steps per epoch — ~280 steps total. Still short but trainable.

7. **Separate the SFT and DPO phases**: SFT on high-quality Ai-voice examples first, then DPO specifically on identity/adversarial pairs. Don't mix voice training with preference training.

8. **Add system-prompt-ablation eval to training loop**: The eval already tests all 4 conditions. Define success as: none-condition qwen_leak < 30% AND none-condition ai_positive > 10%. Until those thresholds are met, the weights haven't shifted.

**Summary**: soul_v1 confirmed the eval infrastructure works and diagnosed the failure mode precisely. soul_v2 needs ≥500 pairs with hollow_template filtered out, ~50 synthetic no-prompt identity pairs, DPO on adversarial probes, and ≥3 epochs.
